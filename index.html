Biography

Giovanni Nicolazzo is a researcher at Transcrime, Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore, where he completed his PhD in Criminology in 2025. His work focuses on organised and financial crime, criminal infiltration in the legal economy, corruption, sanctions circumvention, and the analysis of ownership structures. He combines criminological research with data-driven methods, network analysis, and risk screening tools to examine how illicit actors use legal entities and cross-border corporate structures.

Giovanni Nicolazzo is a researcher at Transcrime, the Joint Research Centre on Innovation and Crime at Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore in Milan. He obtained his PhD in Criminology in 2025, with research centred on the criminal component of the legal economy and on the ways illicit actors exploit business structures, ownership chains, and legal markets.

His research sits at the intersection of criminology, financial crime analysis, and computational methods. He works on topics such as organised and financial crime, corruption, sanctions circumvention, beneficial ownership, and corporate opacity. A recurring focus of his work is the development of empirical and data-driven approaches to identify risk patterns, reconstruct control relationships, and support the detection of suspicious corporate behaviour.

He has contributed to research and applied projects on sanctions evasion, anti-corruption, ownership transparency, and integrity risks in public procurement and major public works. His work combines academic analysis with tools and methodologies designed to be useful for research, policy, journalism, and operational contexts.

Research areas

My research focuses on the intersection between crime and the legal economy. I am particularly interested in organised and financial crime, criminal infiltration in legitimate business, corruption, sanctions circumvention, ownership structures, beneficial ownership, and the use of data-driven methods for risk detection. Across these areas, I work on ways to make hidden corporate relationships and high-risk patterns more visible and measurable.

Projects

KLEPTOTRACE

KLEPTOTRACE examines high-level corruption, sanctions circumvention, and the corporate structures used to conceal control and move assets across jurisdictions. My contribution has focused on sanctions evasion schemes, ownership analysis, and the development of methodologies to identify control links and circumvention patterns.

Major public projects and integrity

This line of work analyses integrity safeguards in major public projects, with a focus on supply chains, anti-mafia controls, workforce screening, and anomaly indicators. It examines how empirical evidence can strengthen prevention against criminal infiltration in large infrastructure projects.

Ownership structures and corporate risk analytics

I develop methods to reconstruct ownership chains, identify control relationships, and detect risk signals connected to money laundering, corruption, organised crime, and sanctions exposure. This work combines business data, network analysis, and red-flag indicators to support transparency and screening.

Anti-corruption and third-party risk

I work on data-driven approaches to assess corruption and integrity risks arising from third-party relationships, corporate links, and opaque structures. The aim is to translate complex business information into practical indicators for research and compliance use.
