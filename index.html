Short bio

Giovanni Nicolazzo is a researcher at Transcrime, Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore, where he completed his PhD in Criminology in 2025. His work focuses on organised and financial crime, criminal infiltration in the legal economy, corruption, sanctions circumvention, and the analysis of ownership structures. He combines criminological research with data-driven methods, network analysis, and risk screening tools to examine how illicit actors use legal entities and cross-border corporate structures.

Research focus

My research focuses on the intersection between crime and the legal economy. I am particularly interested in organised and financial crime, criminal infiltration in legitimate business, corruption, sanctions circumvention, ownership structures, beneficial ownership, and the use of data-driven methods for risk detection. Across these areas, I work on ways to make hidden corporate relationships and high-risk patterns more visible and measurable.

Areas of work

  • criminal infiltration in the legal economy
  • sanctions circumvention and ownership structures
  • corruption and third-party risk
  • corporate opacity and beneficial ownership
  • data-driven risk detection
