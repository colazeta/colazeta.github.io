Biography

Hey there! I'm Giovanni, a Ph.D. criminology student at Milan's Catholic University of the Sacred Heart and researcher at Transcrime. I revel in the entangled realms of financial crime, corruption, and the shadowy underbelly of the economy.

Over at Transcrime, I'm part of the TOM working group, diving deep into the enigma of business ownership structures. Our mission? Revealing who's really pulling the strings in companies, how they do it, and of course, their connections to the crime world. Quite the thrill, huh?

Big fan of mixing computational social science with criminology. Picture this - AI, network techniques, and machine learning used to crack the crime code. I'm all about open-source info, untapped knowledge, and tech-powered crime-fighting. So, ready to dive into the my humble steps in this exciting world of data-driven criminology? Let's go!

KLEPTOTRACE
I'm the coordinator of this ambitious EU-funded project, whose mission is to strengthen the investigation, tracing, and recovery of assets tied to high-level transnational corruption and sanctioned regimes and entities. How? By leveraging an innovative combination of research, training, and the deployment of data-driven tools. This endeavor is carried out in synergy with a multidisciplinary network of partners that include research centers, law enforcement agencies, anti-corruption agencies, financial intelligence units, civil society, journalists, and business registries. Together, we aim to build a more effective and coordinated response to corruption and economic crime on a global scale.

Criminal Infiltration in Lombardia
As the point person on this project, I'm neck-deep in the complex world of business structures across Lombardia. We're turning every stone - shareholders, beneficial owners, related companies, you name it. We're on the trail, mapping HQs and secondary addresses to catch any links to offshore or blacklisted regions, and of course, any dodgy locations. The end game? To unravel suspicious elements and gauge the risk of underworld infiltration.

DATACROS II
As an analyst in this EU-funded project, I'm diving into the labyrinth of European company ownership structures. I've been developing metrics and a composite indicator to spot risks of collusion, corruption, or money laundering. But we're not just crunching numbers - we've built a platform to cast a wide net over European companies, fusing diverse data to create risk scores. We're also making sure these data are super accessible and investigation-friendly! We've got the backing of law enforcement, anti-corruption and competition authorities from the EU and beyond. With this project, we're committed to exposing shady activities and championing transparency and fair competition in business.

PROMENADE
As an analyst on an exciting EU-funded project, I was part ot the team which helped chart a course through the risks of the maritime industry! We developed a unique risk assessment tool for shipowners and maritime transport companies. By merging business ownership information, satellite tracking of ship movements, and judicial precedents, we provided service able to deal in real time with the maritime industry's risk profile. Fellow sailors in this venture included European maritime agencies, Leonardo, Marine Traffic, and GMV. Together, we successfully navigated the high-risk seas of maritime business!

Antonio Bosisio Giovanni Nicolazzo, Michele Riccardi
April, 2021 In Transcrime Research in Brief
Changes in Ownership of Italian Companies during the Covid-19 Emergency: Trends and Risk Factors

I contributed to an in-depth report conducted by Transcrime, a research center at the Catholic University of the Sacred Heart. Our study, supported by Bureau van Dijk, offered a unique examination of ownership changes within Italian companies throughout the Covid-19 pandemic and its implications for potential criminal infiltration and money laundering. We discovered that the pandemic had inadvertently created opportunities for organized and economic crime to infiltrate the legitimate economy and take control of businesses under duress. Through careful monitoring of these ownership changes, we were able to identify risk factors and anomalies among new beneficial owners and ownership structures indicative of possible money laundering and criminal activity. This work is a starting point for broader analysis, urging for an expanded focus on potential infiltration channels such as private financing, the use of shareholder agreements, and the establishment of new companies, particularly in the post-September 2020 context.
