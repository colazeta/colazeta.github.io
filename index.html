Research focus

The work combines criminological interpretation with structured empirical methods. It examines how ownership and control configurations can be reconstructed, screened, and interpreted to identify risk signals and suspicious participation in the legal economy.

Ownership reconstructionTracing legal entities and shareholding chains to recover effective ownership structures.
Control relationshipsMapping direct and indirect control links between firms, individuals, and intermediaries.
Beneficial ownership analysisAssessing opacity patterns and disclosure gaps across jurisdictions and corporate vehicles.
Network analysisUsing relational data to identify central actors, clusters, and recurrent intermediation patterns.
Empirical screeningBuilding indicators and rule-based checks for large-scale firm-level risk detection.
Risk indicatorsOperationalising signals associated with sanctions circumvention and suspicious market participation.
