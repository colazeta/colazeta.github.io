Giovanni Nicolazzo

Researcher on criminal infiltration, sanctions circumvention and ownership structures.

Transcrime · Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore, Milan

Giovanni Nicolazzo is a researcher at Transcrime, Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore. His work examines how criminal and high-risk actors enter, control and use the legal economy through companies, ownership structures and cross-border corporate chains. He combines criminological research with ownership-chain reconstruction, proxy-based screening, network analysis and risk indicators, with a focus on criminal infiltration, sanctions circumvention, corruption and beneficial ownership.

Research focus

My research has two main strands. The first concerns the criminal component of the legal economy: how different criminal actors participate in, control or exploit legitimate business, and how this can be studied beyond a narrowly mafia-centred lens. The second concerns sanctions circumvention and corporate control: how sanctioned or high-risk actors use ownership chains, intermediary jurisdictions and legal vehicles to preserve control and obscure exposure. Across both strands, I work on methods for reconstructing ownership structures, identifying risk indicators and screening suspicious patterns in business data.
